# SOP: Medical Emergency

## Severity: MEDIUM-HIGH (Level 3, escalate to Level 4 if life-threatening)

## Trigger Conditions
- Person collapsed or unresponsive in terminal area
- Report of chest pain, difficulty breathing, or loss of consciousness
- Visible injury from fall, accident, or altercation
- Automated defibrillator (AED) activated
- Multiple persons affected (mass casualty indicator)
- Child or infant in medical distress

## Immediate Actions
1. Dispatch nearest first-aid trained officer to scene immediately
2. Call Airport Medical Services (T2: ext 3911, T3: ext 5822) — response target < 4 min
3. Clear surrounding area — ask bystanders to step back (3m radius)
4. Locate nearest AED unit (marked on terminal map) and bring to scene
5. Assign officer to meet and direct ambulance / paramedics to exact location
6. If unresponsive and not breathing — begin CPR if trained; use AED immediately
7. Notify Terminal Duty Manager and document scene

## Do NOT
- Do NOT move an injured person unless they are in immediate danger (fall injury → spinal risk)
- Do NOT leave patient unattended while awaiting medical services
- Do NOT block emergency vehicle access routes
- Do NOT allow media or bystanders to photograph the patient

## Escalation Criteria
- Patient unresponsive, no pulse → Level 4, request SCDF ambulance immediately
- Multiple victims (>3) → Level 4 Mass Casualty protocol, activate all medical stations
- Suspected communicable disease (e.g., haemorrhage, rash) → Level 4, isolate and notify MOH
- Chemical exposure suspected → Level 5, HAZMAT protocol, SCDF notification

## Post-Incident
- Complete Medical Incident Report within 2 hours
- Provide witness list to medical team if required
- Review AED usage and resupply pads/battery if deployed
- CAAS notification if incident occurred in airside zone
- Refer to passenger for follow-up hospital coordination if required
