# SOP: Fire / Smoke / Evacuation

## Severity: HIGH-CRITICAL (Level 4, escalate to Level 5 if spreading)

## Trigger Conditions
- Automatic smoke or heat detector activated
- Fire alarm panel alert in terminal or airside zones
- Visual confirmation of smoke or fire by officer or passenger
- Sprinkler system activated
- Aircraft ground crew reports smoke or fire near aircraft stand
- Kitchen or retail outlet fire in terminal

## Immediate Actions
1. Activate building fire alarm system for the affected zone immediately
2. Notify SCDF via 995 and Airport ARFF (Aircraft Rescue & Fire Fighting) simultaneously
3. Use PA system to announce evacuation — do NOT say "fire" to avoid panic (use code: "Code Red in Zone [X], all persons please proceed to nearest exit")
4. Evacuate all persons from affected zone to muster points (refer to terminal evacuation map)
5. Dispatch officers to guide evacuation and check toilets, storerooms, and hidden areas
6. Deploy fire extinguishers ONLY if fire is small, contained, and officer is trained
7. Ensure lifts are NOT used — direct all persons to staircases
8. Seal airside access points to prevent fire spread to secure zones

## Do NOT
- Do NOT use lifts during evacuation
- Do NOT attempt to fight a large or spreading fire — evacuate immediately
- Do NOT re-enter the building until SCDF gives all-clear
- Do NOT block emergency vehicle access roads or aircraft taxiways
- Do NOT announce "fire" over public PA (use code words to prevent stampede)

## Escalation Criteria
- Fire spreading beyond initial zone → Level 5, full terminal evacuation
- Persons trapped or injured → Level 5, SCDF USAR team activation
- Aircraft or fuel involved → Level 5, ARFF major incident protocol
- Smoke in HVAC/duct system → Level 5, potential full building evacuation required

## Post-Incident
- Complete SCDF Fire Incident Report and submit within 24 hours
- CAAS notification for any airside fire within 4 hours
- Inspect and reset all fire suppression and alarm systems
- Review muster point headcount records for accountability
- Debrief all responding officers and review evacuation timing
