# SOP: Crowd Management / Crowd Surge

## Severity: LOW-MEDIUM (Level 2-3)

## Trigger Conditions
- Crowd density exceeds 4 persons per square meter in any terminal area
- Queuing system breakdown causing uncontrolled passenger accumulation
- Flight disruption (mass cancellation, delay) causing sudden crowd formation
- Special event arrival/departure causing unusual passenger volumes
- Crowd movement anomaly detected by video analytics (sudden acceleration, reverse flow)
- Report of panic, pushing, or crowd crush conditions

## Immediate Actions
1. Deploy additional officers to affected area immediately — minimum 3 for crowd over 200 persons
2. Open additional processing lanes at check-in, immigration, or security as applicable
3. Use crowd diversion messaging via PA: "Passengers are advised to use [alternate route/area]"
4. Use physical barriers to create orderly queuing channels if needed
5. Contact CAG Operations to request additional terminal support staff
6. Notify airline handlers to accelerate check-in if flight-related
7. Monitor via CCTV — if density increases or crowd becomes agitated → escalate immediately

## Do NOT
- Do NOT use force to disperse a crowd unless public safety is at immediate risk
- Do NOT make announcements that could cause additional panic
- Do NOT create bottlenecks by closing exits or reducing flow points
- Do NOT leave crowd monitoring position during peak density periods

## Escalation Criteria
- Crowd becomes hostile or surge causes falls/injuries → Level 3, request all available officers
- Crowd density causes blockage of emergency exits → Level 3, SCDF notification required
- Suspected deliberate crowd manipulation (distraction technique) → Level 4, security sweep
- Medical emergency within the crowd → Level 3-4, trigger Medical Emergency SOP simultaneously

## Post-Incident
- Document peak crowd density estimates, time, and resolution
- Review video analytics thresholds and alerting triggers
- Report to CAG Terminal Management for capacity planning
- If medical incidents occurred: complete separate Medical Incident Reports
- Review staffing allocation model for high-density periods
