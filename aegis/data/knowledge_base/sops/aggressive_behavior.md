# SOP: Aggressive / Disruptive Passenger Behavior

## Severity: MEDIUM (Level 3)

## Trigger Conditions
- Verbal altercation between passengers or passenger and staff
- Physical threats made against airport staff or other passengers
- Passenger refuses lawful instructions from security or immigration officers
- Individual appears intoxicated and is causing disturbance
- Domestic dispute or family conflict escalating in terminal
- Passenger attempting to force their way through security or boarding
- Staff member reports feeling threatened or harassed

## Immediate Actions
1. Dispatch nearest officer to the scene — maintain calm professional demeanor
2. Separate the involved parties (minimum 5-meter distance between parties)
3. Attempt verbal de-escalation: use calm tone, acknowledge concerns, offer solutions
4. Identify the individual — request travel document or identification
5. Warn individual that continued aggression may result in removal from airport or arrest
6. If airline staff involved: coordinate with airline ground handler to manage disruption
7. If physical contact occurs or is threatened: request APD attendance immediately

## Do NOT
- Do NOT match the individual's aggressive tone or body language
- Do NOT physically restrain without proper justification and training certification
- Do NOT leave an aggressive individual unaccompanied if APD has been called
- Do NOT allow disruption to continue at gate or boarding area (flight safety risk)
- Do NOT engage alone with a physically threatening individual — wait for backup

## Escalation Criteria
- Physical assault occurs → Level 4, APD attendance required, victim medical check
- Weapon produced or threatened → Level 5, Active Threat SOP, evacuate area
- Individual attempts to breach airside → Level 4, Unauthorized Access SOP
- Intoxicated passenger on airside or near aircraft → Level 4, airline + APD immediate notification
- Civil unrest involving multiple passengers → Level 4, crowd management and APD

## Post-Incident
- Complete Disruptive Passenger Report per IATA guidelines
- Coordinate with airline on offloading decision if passenger is on a flight
- APD to handle formal arrest procedures if applicable
- Witness statements collected from staff members involved
- ICA flagging for passenger if deportation or immigration action required
